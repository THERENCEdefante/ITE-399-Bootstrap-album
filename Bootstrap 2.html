Dog breeds and their temperament

Dogs are lovable and loyal beings they can be your companion and protector, they can sometimes be even more human than actual people. This album are here to inform you about the most popular types and breeds of dogs.

Siberian Husky

The Siberian husky is a medium-sized dog, slightly longer than tall. Height ranges from 20 to 23 1/2 inches and weight from 35 to 60 pounds.

The Siberian husky has erect ears and eyes of brown to blue or maybe even one of each color.

Golden retriever

Golden retrievers are outgoing, trustworthy, and eager-to-please family dogs, and relatively easy to train.

They take a joyous and playful approach to life and maintain this puppyish behavior into adulthood.

Great Dane

The Great Dane is a large sized dog breed originating from Germany. The Great Dane descends from hunting dogs from the Middle Ages used to hunt wild boar and deer, and as guardians of German nobility. It is one of the two largest dog breeds in the world, along with the Irish Wolfhound

Pomeranian

Pomeranians are known for being smart, curious, energetic, feisty, and bold. They are typically very playful and love to be the center of attention.

They can make great pets for families but may not be the best choice for those who have small children.

Chihuahua

They like attention and are loyal to their owners. Even though they are considered lap dogs, they are active and like to be kept occupied.

But they have a reputation for snapping at strangers or small children who may be threatening to their diminutive size.

Corgi

Among the most agreeable of all small housedogs, the Pembroke Welsh Corgi is a strong, athletic.

They are a lively little herder who is affectionate and companionable without being needy. They are one the world's most popular herding breeds, Corgis love to be involved in the family.
